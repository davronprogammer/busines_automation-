Dataset details

What is inside the CSV

Columns

  • company_name: Evos, Oqtepa Lavash, Max Way, KFC, Feed Up
  • order_time: random datetime within 30 days
  • product_name: Lavash, Burger, Pizza, Hot-dog, Fries, Cola
  • price: realistic range from 15,000 to 40,000 UZS
  • quantity: 1 to 5 units
  • total_price: price multiplied by quantity
  • customer_count: 1 to 3 customers per order
  • day_of_week and hour: extracted from order time

Behavior built into the synthetic data

  • Lunch hours 11:00 to 14:00 and dinner hours 18:00 to 21:00 were weighted to generate more orders.
  • Weekends were modeled to have higher demand intensity than weekdays.
  • Lavash and Burger were intentionally made the most popular menu categories.
  • The dataset was cleaned for duplicates, converted to proper types, and expanded with engineered features such as is_weekend, time_period, and avg_order_value.

Insights

Business interpretation

1. Lavash and Burger dominate the market

These two products clearly lead both unit volume and revenue. They should be the center of menu planning, campaign design, and supply chain prioritization.

2. Lunch is the strongest commercial moment

Demand is heaviest around midday, especially from 11:00 to 14:00. Fast service during that window likely matters more than late-night availability.

3. Weekends are stronger on a per-day basis

Weekend days average 503 orders and 30.76M UZS per day, while weekdays average 272 orders and 16.67M UZS per day. Weekend operations should therefore be more heavily staffed.

4. Revenue leadership is clear

Evos is the top-performing company in this synthetic market. The next closest brands, Oqtepa Lavash and Max Way, remain competitive but lag behind in total revenue.
